Our Strategic Approach

Our department goals are organized around an integrated Student Lifecycle Framework. We strategically support students from their first discovery of our programs through their successful launch into professional careers‚Äîall powered by a foundation of faculty innovation and scholarship that ensures our curriculum remains cutting-edge and our teaching methods exemplary.

The Student Lifecycle Integration Model

Framework Philosophy

This framework positions our department as architecturally sophisticated and student-centered. Each phase of the student journey is intentionally designed to build upon the previous one, creating a seamless progression from recruitment through career placement. Faculty innovation serves as the engine that powers all three phases, ensuring continuous improvement and relevance.

Scholarship & Teaching Excellence Powers All Phases

Attract Phase

Recruitment & Outreach

  • Grow BAIS enrollment by attracting new students to SBS and promoting double majors and minors across the school.
  • Host alumni-led info sessions for prospective students.
  • Pilot a short "warm-up" experience (e.g., Excel micro-badge).
Develop Phase

Retention & Student Experience

  • Expand project-based learning by developing and promoting the faculty-led consulting project course (ISOM 424).
  • Explore an "innovation incubator" for hands-on experience.
  • Continue the seminar series linking students with alumni and industry leaders.
Launch Phase

Placement & Partnerships

  • Position course projects as portfolio-ready artifacts that students can showcase in the job market.
  • Partner with employers on "interview sprints" and preparation checkpoints.
  • Strengthen alumni mentorship, networking, and placement support.
  • Develop a "skills snapshot" aligning courses with industry needs.
Foundation Layer

Faculty Scholarship & Innovation

  • Pilot faculty development workshops on GenAI/ML and teaching innovation, open to all SBS faculty.
  • Foster collaboration by sharing research ideas and teaching strategies in department exchanges.
  • Support external funding and cross-disciplinary research.

Measuring Our Success

We are committed to tracking meaningful, equitable metrics that capture true impact across the student lifecycle. Our measurement approach balances quantitative outcomes with qualitative insights, ensuring we serve all students effectively.

Our Measurement Philosophy: The 3E Approach

Equity

Disaggregate data to ensure all students succeed

Engagement

Measure depth and quality of student involvement

Effectiveness

Track outcomes that matter for student success

ü§ù Equity Commitment

All metrics will be disaggregated by gender, race/ethnicity, first-generation status, transfer status, and prior experience level. We will actively monitor success gaps and implement targeted interventions to ensure equitable outcomes for all students.

Building Our Shared Vision

We are gathering faculty input so that these goals represent a shared vision for the department. We recognize that certain goals will require multi-year effort.
