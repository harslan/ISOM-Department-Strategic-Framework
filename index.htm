Our Strategic Approach

Our department goals are organized around an integrated Student Lifecycle Framework. We strategically support students from their first discovery of our programs through their successful launch into professional careers‚Äîall powered by a foundation of faculty innovation and scholarship that ensures our curriculum remains cutting-edge and our teaching methods exemplary.

Our Dual Commitment: Student Success & Program Excellence

This framework recognizes that student success and program excellence are mutually reinforcing. Every initiative is designed to directly enhance student learning, career readiness, and belonging‚Äîwhile simultaneously strengthening our department's capacity, reputation, and sustainability. We believe that our students thrive when our program thrives, and our program thrives when our students thrive. Each goal in this framework answers two essential questions: What will students gain or experience? and How does this strengthen our department's ability to serve them even better?

The Student Lifecycle Integration Model

Framework Philosophy

This framework positions our department as architecturally sophisticated and student-centered. Each phase of the student journey is intentionally designed to build upon the previous one, creating a seamless progression from recruitment through career placement. Faculty innovation serves as the engine that powers all three phases, ensuring continuous improvement and relevance.

üéì Faculty Innovation Engine

Scholarship & Teaching Excellence Powers All Phases

Attract Phase

Recruitment & Outreach

  • Grow BAIS enrollment through personalized engagement with prospective students who have already expressed interest in the major, ensuring they receive targeted information and support to make informed decisions while building a qualified, committed student pipeline.
    Student Impact: Personalized guidance for interested students | Program Benefit: Higher conversion of interested prospects
  • Expand access to BAIS education for students across SBS by promoting double majors and minors, helping more students discover technology careers while growing our enrollment through new students rather than cannibalizing other programs.
    Student Impact: More pathways to discover BAIS | Program Benefit: Increased enrollment and SBS partnerships
  • Connect prospective students with alumni mentors through info sessions who share authentic career insights and answer questions, building student confidence in their path while strengthening our alumni engagement network.
    Student Impact: Real career guidance from professionals | Program Benefit: Stronger alumni connections and recruitment
  • Lower barriers to entry with a short "warm-up" experience (e.g., Excel micro-badge) that builds student confidence and skills before committing to the major, while identifying highly motivated prospective students.
    Student Impact: Risk-free way to explore BAIS | Program Benefit: Better retention and student-program fit
Develop Phase

Retention & Student Experience

  • Give students hands-on, real-world consulting experience through an expanded faculty-led project course (ISOM 424), building their portfolios and practical skills while establishing our reputation for applied, experiential learning.
    Student Impact: Portfolio-worthy consulting projects | Program Benefit: Differentiated curriculum and employer recognition
  • Create opportunities for students to develop entrepreneurial and innovation skills through an "innovation incubator," fostering creativity and problem-solving while positioning ISOM as a hub for student innovation within SBS.
    Student Impact: Space to experiment and create | Program Benefit: Enhanced visibility and cross-disciplinary collaboration
  • Connect students directly with alumni and industry leaders through our seminar series, exposing them to diverse career paths and building professional networks while strengthening our industry relationships.
    Student Impact: Career insights and networking opportunities | Program Benefit: Stronger industry partnerships and placement pipelines
Launch Phase

Placement & Partnerships

  • Empower students to showcase their capabilities confidently by positioning course projects as portfolio-ready artifacts, giving them tangible evidence of their skills while demonstrating our program's practical outcomes to employers.
    Student Impact: Professional portfolio for job applications | Program Benefit: Visible outcomes that attract students and employers
  • Prepare students for competitive job markets through employer-partnered "interview sprints" and preparation checkpoints, reducing anxiety and building confidence while strengthening our employer partnerships and placement rates.
    Student Impact: Interview readiness and reduced stress | Program Benefit: Higher placement rates and employer satisfaction
  • Support students' career transitions through enhanced alumni mentorship, networking events, and placement support, ensuring they have guidance when they need it most while building a stronger alumni community.
    Student Impact: Mentorship and career guidance | Program Benefit: Active alumni network and placement success stories
  • Help students articulate their value to employers by developing a "skills snapshot" that maps coursework to industry requirements, boosting their confidence in interviews while ensuring our curriculum stays market-relevant.
    Student Impact: Clear language to describe skills | Program Benefit: Market-aligned curriculum and employer credibility
Foundation Layer

Faculty Scholarship & Innovation

  • Ensure students learn from faculty at the cutting edge of AI and teaching innovation through professional development workshops on GenAI/ML open to all SBS faculty (following Marketing's collaborative model), keeping our curriculum current while building faculty expertise and fostering cross-school collaboration.
    Student Impact: Learning from AI-literate faculty | Program Benefit: Faculty development, SBS leadership positioning, and cross-school collaboration
  • Enhance the student learning experience through faculty collaboration on research ideas and teaching strategies in department exchanges, bringing fresh insights into the classroom while building departmental cohesion and innovation culture.
    Student Impact: Better teaching and diverse perspectives | Program Benefit: Stronger department culture and pedagogical innovation
  • Enrich student learning with research-informed teaching by supporting external funding and cross-disciplinary faculty research, exposing students to cutting-edge thinking while enhancing our scholarly reputation and resources.
    Student Impact: Access to latest research and thinking | Program Benefit: Enhanced reputation and funding resources

Measuring Our Success

We are committed to tracking meaningful, equitable metrics that capture true impact across the student lifecycle. Our measurement approach balances quantitative outcomes with qualitative insights, ensuring we serve all students effectively while building program strength.

Our Measurement Philosophy: The 3E Approach

Equity

Disaggregate data to ensure all students succeed

Engagement

Measure depth and quality of student involvement

Effectiveness

Track outcomes that matter for student and program success

ü§ù Equity Commitment

All metrics will be disaggregated by gender, race/ethnicity, first-generation status, transfer status, and prior experience level. We will actively monitor success gaps and implement targeted interventions to ensure equitable outcomes for all students.

üéØ Attract Phase Metrics

Enrollment Growth & Access
Total BAIS majors, double major/minor adoption rates, and demographic diversity (first-gen, underrepresented groups, transfer students)
Pathway Effectiveness
Inquiry-to-enrollment conversion rate; micro-badge completion ‚Üí course enrollment rate
Student Awareness & Understanding
Alumni session participation and satisfaction; pre/post survey: "How well do you understand BAIS career opportunities?"

üöÄ Develop Phase Metrics

Student Retention & Progress
Major retention rate; course completion rates; credit momentum (on-time progression)
Experiential Learning Participation
ISOM 424 enrollment and project completion; innovation incubator participation; seminar series attendance (unique + repeat)
Student Learning & Satisfaction
Feedback: "This course gave me hands-on, real-world experience"; pre/post skill confidence; faculty project quality assessments

üíº Launch Phase Metrics

Career Success Outcomes
Employment/grad school rate at 6 months; job relevance score: "My position utilizes my ISOM education"; starting salary ranges (contextualized)
Student Career Readiness
% students with portfolio artifacts; interview sprint participation; pre/post: "I feel prepared to interview for roles in my field"
Support Network Strength
Mentor-mentee matches and relationship duration (3+ months); alumni engagement rate; skills snapshot adoption and student confidence

üéì Foundation Layer Metrics

Faculty Development & Innovation
Workshop participation (ISOM + other SBS faculty); teaching innovation adoption rate; cross-departmental collaborations
Scholarship & Research Impact
Publications and presentations; external funding (submitted and awarded); cross-disciplinary research projects
Faculty Sustainability
Survey: "I have adequate support for professional development"; workload equity across ranks; faculty retention rate
